# The Constitution of 7fchain

*A short statement of the principles that govern the chain and the people who run it.*

---

## Preamble

We the miners, node runners, and users of 7fchain, in order to form a more perfect settlement layer, secure the property of those who hold value upon it, provide for the common defense against those who would attack or capture it, give every willing participant a fair shot at mining a block and running a meaningful node, and distribute the work and the reward across the whole of the earth, do ordain and establish this Constitution for 7fchain.

We build in the ethos of Satoshi: sound money, public verifiability, and a network owned by the people who run it — carried forward into the post-quantum era, where every signature is built to outlast the machine that would break it.

---

## Article I — The Miners

The miners are the electorate of 7fchain. Block production, not capital, is the franchise.

The network seats up to **70,000 active miner certificates** at any one time — citizens of the chain. Over a roughly two-year horizon, toward block 140,000, the rotation rules are designed so that block production spreads across a vast number of distinct miners rather than concentrating in a handful. The chain guarantees no one a block; it guarantees everyone a fair shot at one. When the active roster is full, the chain does not close its doors: new participants are admitted with one-shot certificates, so that anyone who wishes to mine a single block may always do so.

Three rules keep the franchise broad and equal:

1. **Proof of work for the common machine.** Mining runs on consumer CPUs. The proof of work is built to give no advantage to ASICs, GPUs, or data centers; the gap between the largest and smallest honest operator is measured in dozens, not billions. An old laptop or a twenty-dollar server is a meaningful vote, and no amount of specialized hardware makes one miner's vote worth more.
2. **Cooldown rotation.** After a certificate mines a block, it must stand aside while others take their turn. No quantity of hash power buys a second consecutive turn. The reward distributes across the active community, not into pools.
3. **A lifetime cap per certificate.** Each certificate may produce only a bounded number of blocks across its life. No single seat may dominate, and the early citizens are not paid disproportionately to those who come after.

No miner is greater than any other miner. This is the egalitarian heart of the chain.

## Article II — Admission to the Union

7fchain is a **voluntarily entered ecosystem**. No one is conscripted, and no one is excluded who is willing to play by the rules.

Admission is by certificate, issued through a worldwide network of regional partners so that participation is **distributed geographically around the world** and no single nation or region holds the chain. The certificate is the ticket of entry and the badge of citizenship; it is open, inexpensive, and renewable. Its small cost is not a toll on the poor but a wall against the Sybil — an attacker must pay in proportion to the harm he intends.

Those who keep the rules keep their place. Those who attack the network, violate the cooldown, or act in bad faith may have their certificates revoked under a published, auditable policy. The door is open to all who enter in good faith; it closes only behind those who break the compact.

## Article III — The Right to Property

The first purpose of the chain is to **secure property rights**.

Value held on 7fchain belongs to the holder of the keys, and to no one else. Every coin is locked by a post-quantum signature designed to remain unforgeable in the age of the quantum computer. No miner, no operator, no founder, and no certificate authority can move, freeze, or seize what an honest holder owns. A stolen network identity cannot redirect another's reward; a compromised operator key cannot take an owner's coin.

What you hold, you hold. The chain exists to make that promise true and to keep it true against the most powerful adversary we can foresee.

## Article IV — The Common Defense

A young chain must survive its infancy. 7fchain is built to **protect itself in its early days from attack**, and to keep protecting itself as it grows.

The certificate gate authenticates every peer and makes hostile flooding expensive. The cooldown and the per-certificate cap deny any actor a dominant share of block production. Revocation removes proven bad actors without closing the network. And the chain anchors its history to Bitcoin's proof of work on a continuous basis, so that its past is timestamped into the most secure ledger on earth and cannot be quietly rewritten.

Defense is layered and deliberate: cryptographic, economic, and procedural at once. The aim is a network that is hard to centralize, hard to capture, and hard to break.

## Article V — The Security Tax

The chain, like a country, must fund its own defense and upkeep.

Each block dedicates **one percent** of its reward and its fees to the Seven Fortunas developer fund — the standing budget that pays for the cryptography, the certificate authority, the monitoring, and the continued building that keep the common defense and the common infrastructure alive for everyone. Ninety-nine percent goes to the miner who did the work. This one percent can be thought of as the chain's security tax: perpetual, transparent, enforced at consensus, and paid by economic activity in proportion to it. It is the chain's only levy. No tax is levied on the value a holder holds or transfers.

## Article VI — Layer One Anchors, Layer Two Lives

Layer 1 is deliberately small, slow, and sound. Its transactions are not meant to be the daily commerce of the people. **The role of Layer 1, and of Layer 1 transactions, is to anchor the Layer 2.**

Upon this settled foundation we build Layer 2 networks that are **highly functional and fast, able to handle any number of use cases** — remittance for working families, stable money, payments between people and the AI agents they authorize, and whatever honest commerce comes next. The mission gets real on Layer 2. Layer 1 is the bedrock beneath it: scarce, secure, and quantum-resistant, holding the whole structure to a public and immovable record.

## Article VII — The Ethos and Its Amendment

This chain keeps the ethos of Satoshi and adds nothing that betrays it: **no meme coins, no spam, no scams** — only honest utility, sound and scarce money, and a network run by those who believe in it. Its issuance is disciplined and decays toward a permanent, modest tail — roughly one percent monetary inflation by its thirtieth decay period (about calendar year 28) and around 0.07 percent at the floor, well beneath gold's historic ≈1.5 percent — so that the security of the chain never starves and its money is never debased. Its founders are paid only as the community succeeds, on the same mining terms as everyone else.

These principles are the chain's constant. The mechanisms that serve them may be amended by the rough consensus of those who run the network, in the open, and never in a direction that narrows the franchise, weakens a holder's claim to his own property, or abandons the people for whom this chain was built.
